Silver Lining


---------------------------------------------------------
I have gone through the effort of permenently transcoding my movies collection into a format that will play on practically any device.

The format is as follows:
* container: .mp4
* video codec: AVC (h.264) 8/10 bit SDR
* audio codec: AAC and sometimes Dolby surround (optional)
* subtitles: .srt or .ass or mov_text
---------------------------------------------------------
These are old well established codecs, with a ton of support. And should play on
** chrome/firefox/edge
** xbox/playstation
** android TV
** Roku TV
** amazon fire TV

Non-Silver Lining... Coal Lining?


---------------------------------------------------------
I have semi-standardized Television into the following format

* container: .mkv
* video codec: HEVC (h.265) mostly 10 bit/some 8 bit SDR
* audio codec: EAC3/AC3/and a few OPUS
* Subtitles: .srt or .ass or mov_text
---------------------------------------------------------
This is a newer standard, much more efficient and higher quality vs file size. And it is also future proof, meaning this is what devices and streaming services are moving towards.

With the obvious trade-off of it being less supported on older devices and hardware.
---------------------------------------------------------
To the best of my knowledge. This should play on most things, but here is a rough guide from what I can tell from online.
----- COMPATIBILITY -----
xbox one series S: Full compatibility (except for old anamorphic video, which i am converting slowly). Personally tested (what i use)
playstation 5:i dont know, but im guessing same as xbox 1 s

edge: requires hevc codec from microsoft store. then should work i think
safarimkv with hevc = no good
android TV/amazon fireyes; with android 5.0+ ; however early amazon fire may not, device dependant
swiftfin (iOS): yes with iOS 14 and A8X chip or newer
roku: hevc only supported on 4k devices
kodi with jellyfin plugin:yes; full compatibility
jellyfin desktop client:yes; full compatibility

Asides from my xbox and google chrome, and an "amazon fire stick 4k select", I haven't tested the others.
