Silver Lining


---------------------------------------------------------
I have gone through the effort of permenently transcoding my movies collection into a format that will play on practically any device.

The format is as follows:
* container: .mp4
* video codec: AVC (h.264) 8/10 bit SDR
* audio codec: AAC and sometimes Dolby surround (optional)
* subtitles: .srt or .ass or mov_text
---------------------------------------------------------
These are old well established codecs, with a ton of support. And should play on .... honestly anything I can think of.
** chrome/firefox/edge
** xbox/playstation
** android TV
** Roku TV
** amazon fire TV

Non-Silver Lining... Coal Lining?


---------------------------------------------------------
I have semi-standardized Television into the following format (still a work in progress)

* container: .mkv
* video codec: HEVC (h.265) mostly 10 bit/some 8 bit SDR
* audio codec: EAC3/AC3/and a few OPUS
* Subtitles: .srt or .ass or mov_text
---------------------------------------------------------
This is a newer standard, much more efficient and higher quality vs file size. And it is also future proof, meaning this is what devices and streaming services are moving towards.

With the obvious trade-off of it being less supported on older devices and hardware.
---------------------------------------------------------
Support for these codecs can be a bit hit and miss. Here is a rough guide from what I can tell from online as well as some I have tested myself.
----- COMPATIBILITY -----
jellyfin desktop client:yes; full compatibility; recommended for computers/laptops
xbox one series S: yes; Full compatibility (except for old anamorphic video, which i am converting slowly). Personally tested (what i use)
playstation 5:i dont know, but im guessing same as xbox 1 s
chrome: no; doesn't like a lot of EAC3 files.
firefox: no; does not play mkv files or AC3 audio
edge: probably no; requires hevc codec from microsoft store, idk about sound
safarino; mkv with hevc = no good
android TV/amazon firemost; with android 5.0+ ; however early amazon fire may not, device dependant
swiftfin (iOS): most; with iOS 14 and A8X chip or newer
roku: some; hevc only supported on 4k devices. EAC3/AC3; probably? OPUS; yes
kodi with jellyfin plugin:yes; I think. may depend some on device with kodi on it

Asides from my xbox/the major browsers, I haven't tested the others.
So I'd say look up the specifications for your particular device, or if you would like. You can watch 2-3 minutes of the following files and ask me.
I will look in my servers log files wether it had to transcode for you or if they were compatible.

12 Monkeys [S01E01] - mkv. HEVC 8 bit SDR. AC3 - 6 ch.
